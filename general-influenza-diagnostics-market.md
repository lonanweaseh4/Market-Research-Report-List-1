<p><p>The general influenza diagnostics market encompasses several testing methods. Rapid Influenza Diagnostic Tests (RIDT) provide quick results by detecting viral antigens. Viral culture involves growing the influenza virus from a patient sample, enabling detailed strain analysis but requiring more time. Direct Fluorescent Antibody (DFA) assays detect viral proteins in respiratory specimens, offering moderate speed and accuracy. Serological assays identify antibodies in blood, assessing past infections. Together, these methods cater to diverse clinical needs and enhance influenza management strategies.</p></p>

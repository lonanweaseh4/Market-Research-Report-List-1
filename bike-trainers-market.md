<p><p>The bike trainer market features two primary types: classic trainers and smart trainers. Classic trainers use a resistance mechanism, allowing cyclists to ride in place while simulating road conditions, typically lacking connectivity. In contrast, smart trainers integrate technology, offering interactive features like connectivity to cycling apps, adjustable resistance, and virtual rides, enhancing the indoor cycling experience. Smart trainers cater to tech-savvy cyclists seeking immersive training, while classic trainers appeal to those preferring simplicity and straightforward functionality.</p></p>
